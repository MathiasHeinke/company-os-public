## 1. Purpose And Non-Goals

The 0.6C Social Approval Queue is a **text-first queue specification**. It defines
all states a social post draft passes through inside Company.OS, all transitions
between those states, who owns each transition, how the scheduling intent card
reaches a human approver, which external scheduling actions are human-mediated,
which are forbidden, the staleness rule for unapproved drafts, and the
no-autonomous-external-scheduling rule with its refusal template.

Social content means: posts drafted for LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Threads, Bluesky,
Mastodon, and any other public social platform the operator designates.

This spec is explicitly NOT:

- a scheduler, posting engine, or API adapter
- a connector to Buffer, Hypefury, Make, or any external scheduling service
- an authorization layer for direct posting, outreach sends, or spend
- a CMS integration or cross-channel distribution layer
- a department pack (those land in `0.8.x-beta` per
  `docs/strategy/autonomy-product-horizon.md`)
- authorization for any public post or HG-2.5 release — releases remain
  CEO/Founder-owned per `docs/governance/ceo-release-authority.md`

`FACT(docs/releases/0.6-growth-beta-entry-plan.md)` lines 33 and 25 — the social
approval queue track has the hard boundary "No scheduling/posting without HG
release" and the overall 0.6 entry condition that "No worker can publish, schedule
external posts, send outreach, change spend, make regulated claims or mutate
production systems without a human gate."

`FACT(docs/strategy/autonomy-product-horizon.md)` lines 93–95 — the `0.6.x-beta`
Growth beta target is "campaigns draft autonomously, publish gated"; autonomous
publishing or scheduling is explicitly out of scope until at minimum `0.7.x-beta`.

`FACT(docs/releases/0.6a-marketing-growth-command-center-map.md)` Section 5 —
external scheduling is listed as a blocked action class (HG-2 scheduler intent card
approved by `role:cmo`); the 0.6C spec concretizes that intent-card boundary.

## 2. Scope Boundaries (Include / Exclude)

Included:

- nine (9) named queue states with stable identifiers
- every allowed state transition with its trigger, actor, and exit criterion
- who can move items between each pair of states
- the human-mediated external scheduler path (intent card → human approval → human
  paste into scheduling tool)
- the explicit list of forbidden scheduler writes
- the 24-hour staleness rule for drafts that stall in `scheduling_intent`
- the no-autonomous-external-scheduling rule and refusal template
- a scheduling intent card schema (chat-message form, pasteable by human into Plane)
- HumanGate map across all queue states
- evidence and source map for every load-bearing claim
- reflection and learning proposals

Excluded:

- live posting on any social platform
- any direct write to Buffer, Hypefury, Make, or any other external scheduling
  service API
- outreach sends, DMs, or direct customer contact
- ad spend changes, ad copy approvals, or ad targeting changes
- production deployment of any kind
- customer data access
- direct Plane state mutation (no `Done`, no status flips, no comment writes other
  than the gated worker.reported)
- platform-specific formatting adapters (character limits, image specs, hashtag
  lists) — those are scheduling-tool concerns, out of scope for 0.6C

## 3. Queue State Map

### 3.1 All States

The social approval queue has nine stable states. Every social post draft that
enters the queue begins in `draft`. States `external_scheduler` and `withdrawn`
are terminal.

| # | State ID | Description | Terminal? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | `draft` | Draft variants written and saved under the allowed write path; initial state | No |
| Q2 | `editorial` | Under voice, tone, structure, and platform-fit review by editorial reviewer (must differ from writer) | No |
| Q3 | `claim_review` | Claims audited for regulated content and freshness by `role:cao` (separate session) + `role:cmo` sign-off | No |
| Q4 | `scheduling_intent` | Scheduling intent card generated and presented to human `role:cmo` for approval; staleness clock starts on entry | No |
| Q5 | `human_approved` | Human `role:cmo` seat has approved the scheduling intent card; draft cleared for scheduler handoff | No |
| Q6 | `external_scheduler` | Human operator has manually pasted the approved post into the external scheduling tool; success terminal state | **Yes** |
| Q7 | `stale` | Draft was in `scheduling_intent` for more than 24 hours without human approval; routed back to `editorial` for freshness review | No (see §4) |
| Q8 | `rejected` | Failed editorial or claim gate with a documented verdict; returned to writer for revision | No |
| Q9 | `withdrawn` | Explicitly withdrawn by human operator at any stage; end of queue lifecycle | **Yes** |

### 3.2 State Transition Table

Every transition is named, gated, and has a single responsible actor. No
transition may happen without a gate verdict or an explicit human action.

| From | To | Trigger | Actor | Gate / Exit Criterion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `draft` | `editorial` | Writer submits draft for review | `role:cmo` writer-agent or operator | Draft has: at least one post variant per target platform, a platform label, a parent Plane item ID or topic note, and a "fits voice/doctrine" self-check answered against `kits/company-os-kit/.agents/personas/` |
| `editorial` | `draft` | Editorial reviewer issues `editorial_reject` | `role:cmo` editorial reviewer (must differ from writer) | Reviewer produces `editorial-review.md` verdict artifact naming each failed dimension; writer must address all items before re-submitting |
| `editorial` | `claim_review` | Editorial reviewer issues `editorial_pass` | `role:cmo` editorial reviewer (must differ from writer) | All editorial dimensions pass (see §5); reviewer produces signed `editorial-review.md` artifact |
| `claim_review` | `editorial` | CAO or CMO issues `claim_reject` | `role:cao` (claim gate) or `role:cmo` (ordinary copy freshness) | `claim-review.md` artifact names every failed claim or stale fact; draft must be revised before re-entering `editorial` |
| `claim_review` | `scheduling_intent` | CAO and CMO issue joint `claim_pass` | `role:cao` + `role:cmo` sign-off | `claim-review.md` artifact names each claim with evidence path and verdict; staleness clock starts at this transition |
| `scheduling_intent` | `human_approved` | Human `role:cmo` seat manually approves the scheduling intent card | Human `role:cmo` seat only | Human has reviewed the full scheduling intent card (platform, timing, copy, evidence), signed the approval in the Plane comment, and confirmed the target external scheduler |
| `scheduling_intent` | `editorial` (via `stale`) | Staleness rule fires (24 hours elapsed without approval) | Automated dispatcher rule; human notified | See §4 for the full staleness rule; editorial reviewer must re-run freshness and platform-fit check |
| `scheduling_intent` | `withdrawn` | Human `role:cmo` explicitly rejects the scheduling intent card | Human `role:cmo` seat only | Human documents rejection reason in Plane comment; draft may be archived or revised |
| `human_approved` | `external_scheduler` | Human operator manually pastes approved post into external scheduling tool | Human operator only (no autonomous write to any scheduler API) | Operator confirms the post is queued in the external tool; posts a `human_gate.released` event in Plane with tool name and scheduled time |
| Any non-terminal | `withdrawn` | Operator explicitly withdraws at any point | Human `role:cmo` operator | Operator documents withdrawal reason in Plane comment; queue item is closed |

### 3.3 Who Can Move Items Between States

| Transition | Required Actor | Forbidden Actors |
|---|---|---|
| `draft` → `editorial` | `role:cmo` writer-agent or operator | CAO, CEO, any autonomous tool, chat surface without operator confirmation |
| `editorial` → `draft` | `role:cmo` editorial reviewer (different person/agent from writer) | The writer who produced the draft; any automated rule |
| `editorial` → `claim_review` | `role:cmo` editorial reviewer (different person/agent from writer) | The writer who produced the draft; any automated rule |
| `claim_review` → `editorial` | `role:cao` (claim gate) or `role:cmo` (freshness) | CEO, writer, any automated posting or scheduling tool |
| `claim_review` → `scheduling_intent` | `role:cao` + `role:cmo` joint approval | Writer, CEO (from this gate), any autonomous tool |
| `scheduling_intent` → `human_approved` | Human `role:cmo` seat only — a human, not an agent | Any agent, any automated rule, CAO, CEO |
| `scheduling_intent` → `editorial` (stale) | Dispatcher rule fires automatically; human is notified but does not initiate the transition | Human may not block the staleness transition once the 24h clock has expired |
| `scheduling_intent` → `withdrawn` | Human `role:cmo` seat only | Any agent, any automated rule |
| `human_approved` → `external_scheduler` | Human operator only — pasting manually into the external tool | Any agent, any API call, any cron trigger, any automated dispatch |
| Any → `withdrawn` | Human `role:cmo` operator | Any agent acting without operator instruction |

## 4. The 24-Hour Staleness Rule

**Rule:** Any social post draft that enters `scheduling_intent` and has not
received a human approval (`human_approved`) within 24 hours of entry
MUST be automatically reclassified as `stale` and routed back to `editorial`
for a mandatory freshness and platform-fit review.

**Rationale:** Social content degrades quickly. A post written about a
breaking trend, a product launch, or a time-sensitive moment may be
actively harmful or misleading if published stale. The 24-hour window
prevents the queue from accumulating approved-but-cold posts that have
silently lost their relevance.

**Clock start:** The staleness clock starts at the moment the draft
transitions from `claim_review` to `scheduling_intent`. The dispatcher
records this timestamp as `staleness_clock_start` in the scheduling
intent card artifact.

**What fires the transition:**
- The Runtime Dispatcher v1.x checks every `scheduling_intent` draft
  on its heartbeat cycle.
- When `now - staleness_clock_start > 24h`, the dispatcher emits a
  `worker.blocked` event with reason `STALE_SCHEDULING_INTENT` and
  routes the draft to `editorial`.
- The editorial reviewer receives a freshness-review prompt, not a
  full re-review. The reviewer confirms: (a) the post is still timely,
  (b) the facts are still accurate, (c) the scheduling context is still
  appropriate. If all three pass, the draft may re-enter `claim_review`
  immediately with the same claim-review artifact (no new CAO session
  unless the content changed materially).

**Re-entry path after stale:**

```text
stale → editorial (freshness-only review)
editorial → claim_review (if content changed materially, full review)
editorial → scheduling_intent (if only timing changed, re-emit intent card)
```

**Human notification:** The human `role:cmo` seat is notified via the
dispatcher heartbeat Plane comment when a draft transitions to `stale`.
The notification names the draft, the original `scheduling_intent` entry
time, and the freshness review required before re-queuing.

**Operator override:** A human `role:cmo` operator may explicitly extend
the staleness window by up to 24 additional hours (total maximum 48h)
by posting an `extend_staleness_window` comment in the Plane work item.
No agent may grant this extension unilaterally.

## 5. Editorial Review Dimensions (Social)

Social editorial review differs from blog editorial review in cadence and
platform sensitivity. The editorial reviewer at the `editorial` stage checks:

- **Voice fit.** Matches local persona files in
  `kits/company-os-kit/.agents/personas/` and any local voice guide.
- **Platform fit.** Post length, register, and link behavior are appropriate
  for the target platform label(s) on the draft.
- **Timing fit.** Draft is timely relative to the topic or announcement
  it references; no stale data or outdated event reference.
- **Claim health.** Every factual claim includes a source path or URL;
  no Honcho-only "remembered fact" citations.
- **Blocked-action compliance.** Draft does not contain a call to action
  that would require spend, outreach, or regulated claim approval.

If any dimension scores `REJECT`, the draft returns to `draft` with a
reviewer-signed artifact naming the failed dimensions. The editorial reviewer
may not approve a draft with known failures.

`INFERENCE(AGENTS.md)` lines 159–168 — "Output quality" and "Work quality"
are evaluated separately; an agent may self-review but not self-approve. The
owner-separation rule from the blog workflow (0.6B §5) applies equally here:
the editorial reviewer must differ from the writer.

## 6. Claim Review Dimensions (Social)

Claim review at `claim_review` always runs, even if the draft contains no
regulated claims. The `role:cao` seat (separate session) audits:

- **Regulated claim detection.** Any claim that falls under the medical,
  legal, Rx, financial outcome, security, or other regulated class per
  `docs/releases/0.6-growth-beta-entry-plan.md` line 34 is flagged.
- **Freshness audit.** Every fact must be verifiably current within the
  freshness window declared on the draft.
- **Source quality.** Every factual claim cites a path or URL; no
  Honcho-only memories cited as facts.

When a regulated claim is detected:

- The `claim_review` state produces a `claim-review.md` artifact with
  `verdict: REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED`.
- The draft pauses at `claim_review` until the Claim/Compliance Gate
  child workflow (0.6D) returns `claim_approved` (Founder, HG-4) or
  `claim_removed` (writer).
- No agent may push a regulated draft to `scheduling_intent` without HG-4.

## 7. External Scheduler Path

### 7.1 Human-Mediated Path (Allowed)

The only allowed path to an external scheduling tool is:

```text
human_approved → human operator manually pastes post into scheduling tool
```

The following external tools are recognized as valid paste destinations
when the operator chooses them:

| Tool | Mode | Authorization required |
|---|---|---|
| Buffer | Manual paste by human operator | HG-2 (scheduling intent card approved by `role:cmo`) |
| Hypefury | Manual paste by human operator | HG-2 (scheduling intent card approved by `role:cmo`) |
| Make (manual trigger) | Human operator triggers the Make scenario manually | HG-2 (scheduling intent card approved by `role:cmo`) |
| Native platform scheduler (LinkedIn, X, etc.) | Human operator schedules directly in the platform UI | HG-2 (scheduling intent card approved by `role:cmo`) |
| Manual post (no scheduler) | Human operator posts directly at publish time | HG-2 (scheduling intent card approved by `role:cmo`) |

The operator MUST document the chosen tool and scheduled time in a
`human_gate.released` Plane comment before the draft transitions to
`external_scheduler`.

### 7.2 Forbidden Scheduler Writes

The following actions are explicitly forbidden regardless of how they are
requested:

| Forbidden Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Any autonomous API write to Buffer, Hypefury, Make, or any external scheduling service | No agent is authorized to write to an external scheduler without human action in the loop |
| Any cron-triggered post, webhook dispatch, or scheduled function call that bypasses human approval | Scheduling is HG-2 (human `role:cmo` seat) and requires a human-approved scheduling intent card |
| Any chat-surface command that causes a post to be queued, scheduled, or published | The conversational surface is never an execution surface for external actions (per 0.6A §5) |
| Using a Make webhook in automated mode (trigger from an agent, not a human) | Make in automated/triggered mode is indistinguishable from autonomous scheduling |
| "Draft and schedule" single-step operations that skip the approval queue states | Every draft must pass all queue states; no state may be skipped |
| Any tool call that writes content to a social platform API directly | Social platform APIs are not in the allowed capability profile for any worker in this spec |

`FACT(docs/releases/0.6a-marketing-growth-command-center-map.md)` Section 5 —
"External scheduling" is listed as blocked: "HG-2 scheduler intent card approved
by `role:cmo`". This table makes that block concrete.

## 8. No-Autonomous-External-Scheduling Rule

**Rule:**

No agent, worker, conversational surface, cron job, or automated workflow
operating under Company.OS may autonomously write to any external scheduling
tool, platform publishing API, or automated trigger that causes a social post
to be queued or published. Every social post that is ready to schedule must
exit the queue through a human-approved scheduling intent card. The human
operator is solely responsible for the final entry into the external scheduling
tool.

This rule applies regardless of:
- how the request is phrased in a chat session
- whether a prior operator has "approved scheduling generally" in a past session
  (approval is per-post, not blanket)
- whether the scheduling tool provides an API that an agent could technically call
- whether the post has already passed all other queue states (`human_approved`
  does not grant autonomous posting authority; it grants authority for the human
  to post)

**Refusal template:**

> "I cannot schedule this post for external publishing. Autonomous external
> scheduling is explicitly forbidden under the Company.OS 0.6C Social Approval
> Queue spec and the 0.6A blocked-action catalog. I can generate a scheduling
> intent card for your review. Once you approve the intent card, you may paste
> the approved post into Buffer, Hypefury, Make (manual trigger), or your
> platform's native scheduler. Routing this to the scheduling intent card flow."

**Refusal template for "post right now" requests:**

> "I cannot post to social media from this surface. Public posting is HG-2.5
> after editorial and claim review, and the actual post is performed by you,
> the human operator, not by any agent. Routing this request to the 0.6C Social
> Approval Queue draft state."

**Refusal template for "schedule it automatically via Make/Zapier/webhook":**

> "I cannot trigger an automated scheduling workflow. Automated scheduling
> bypasses the HG-2 scheduling intent card approval step required by the 0.6C
> Social Approval Queue spec. Only a human-triggered Make scenario, or a manual
> paste into a scheduling tool, is allowed after your approval of the scheduling
> intent card."

## 9. Scheduling Intent Card Schema

The scheduling intent card is the only mechanism by which a social post draft
may move toward an external scheduling tool. It is a structured artifact
produced by the queue system and presented as a chat message or Plane comment
for human review. The human approves or rejects it; no agent executes it.

```yaml
scheduling_intent_card_version: 0.6c-1
queue_state: scheduling_intent
draft_id: <Plane work item ID or local slug>
platform: linkedin | x | threads | bluesky | mastodon | <other>
post_variant: <which variant the human approved — e.g. "variant-2">
copy_hash: <SHA-256 of the approved post copy; post may not change after approval>
staleness_clock_start: <ISO-8601 UTC timestamp when draft entered scheduling_intent>
staleness_deadline: <staleness_clock_start + 24h>
claim_review_artifact: <absolute path to the signed claim-review.md>
editorial_review_artifact: <absolute path to the signed editorial-review.md>
proposed_schedule:
  tool: buffer | hypefury | make-manual | native-platform | manual
  requested_time: <ISO-8601 UTC preferred publish time, or "ASAP" or "operator-chooses">
  timezone_note: <human-readable note for operator>
proposed_scope:
  include:
    - paste approved variant into scheduling tool after human approval
  exclude:
    - no autonomous API call to any scheduling tool
    - no direct posting from this surface
    - no spend change
    - no customer data
    - no Plane Done from worker
proposed_human_gate: HG-2
proposed_human_gate_owner: role:cmo
notes_for_human_reviewer: >
  This scheduling intent card summarizes the approved draft variants, evidence
  chain, and proposed schedule. Review the copy_hash to verify the post copy
  matches the approved editorial and claim artifacts. After you approve this
  card, paste the post into the named scheduling tool manually. No agent will
  take action after your approval.
```

The card is presented as a fenced YAML block. The human reviewer:
1. Verifies the `copy_hash` matches the artifact they reviewed.
2. Confirms the `proposed_schedule.tool` and `requested_time`.
3. Approves by posting an explicit approval comment in the Plane work item
   (not by replying in chat — chat approval is not binding).
4. After approval, pastes the post into the scheduling tool manually.
5. Posts a `human_gate.released` event in Plane naming the tool, the
   scheduled time, and the final approved copy hash.

## 12. Reflection

`reflection:`

- The plan-only mode mapped cleanly to the artifact: this document defines all
  queue states, all transitions, and all ownership rules without dispatching any
  worker, producing any social post, or writing to any external system.
- The hardest design tension was the `scheduling_intent` state. It would have
  been simpler to collapse `human_approved` into `external_scheduler` and let
  the human's paste action be the only terminal step. Instead, the two states
  are separated so the system can record the moment of explicit human approval
  independently from the moment of physical scheduling. This separation provides
  a clean audit boundary: the dispatcher can assert "the human approved at T1"
  and "the operator pasted at T2" without ambiguity.
- The 24-hour staleness rule required a clock-start anchor. The choice of
  `scheduling_intent` entry time is deliberate: the scheduling intent card is
  the last artifact fully produced before human action is required. If the
  clock started at `claim_review` exit, the staleness window could be consumed
  by normal human review latency before any scheduling is attempted. Anchoring
  at `scheduling_intent` gives the human a full 24 hours from the moment the
  system says "we are ready for your approval."
- The operator-extension cap (48h total maximum) prevents staleness-rule farming:
  an operator could theoretically extend indefinitely to avoid re-reviews. The
  48h hard cap forces a freshness review no later than two days after the
  scheduling intent card was generated, which is the outer bound of reasonable
  social timeliness for planned content.
- The `copy_hash` field in the scheduling intent card closes a drift gap: without
  it, an operator could approve a card and then silently modify the copy before
  pasting. The hash makes the editorial and claim review artifacts point to a
  specific immutable version of the copy.
- No external-scheduler API connection was designed. The `proposed_schedule.tool`
  field names the tool but makes no API call. The human paste is the execution
  surface. This is consistent with the 0.6 text-first doctrine and avoids any
  implicit API connector from sneaking into the spec.
