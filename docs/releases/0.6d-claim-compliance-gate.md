## 1. Purpose And Non-Goals

The 0.6D Claim / Compliance Gate is a **governance gate specification**. It
defines what constitutes a regulated claim inside Company.OS, what evidence each
regulated class requires before any draft may move past the gate, who owns each
claim class, when Founder approval (HG-4) is mandatory, how the gate logs every
claim review in Plane, and what refuse-with-redirect templates the conversational
surface uses when a regulated claim is requested.

This spec is explicitly NOT:

- an approval of any specific claim, past, present or future
- a drafting surface for customer-facing regulated copy
- a legal review service or licensed legal opinion
- a medical, pharmaceutical, financial or security advisory
- a customer-data-aware system
- a production deployment, external publish, or outreach send
- authorization for any worker to approve regulated claims unilaterally

`FACT(docs/releases/0.6-growth-beta-entry-plan.md)` — the Claim/compliance gate
track boundary is "No medical/legal/regulated claims without explicit gate." This
spec enforces that boundary; it does not widen it.

`FACT(docs/releases/0.6a-marketing-growth-command-center-map.md)` Section 5 —
regulated/public claims are a blocked action class requiring HG-4 (Founder) plus
this Claim/Compliance Gate child workflow.

`FACT(docs/agents/cao.md)` — CAO does not build, does not approve, does not
draft customer-facing copy. The CAO seat's role in this gate is audit-only:
enumerate, classify, and route regulated claims. Approval is Founder.

## 2. Scope Boundaries (Include / Exclude)

Included:

- six (6) regulated claim classes with stable identifiers
- minimum evidence requirement per class before a draft may advance past the gate
- role ownership table per class with HG-4 mandatory escalation mapping
- Plane claim-review log shape (Plane comment YAML) for every claim review
- evidence path convention for claim artifacts
- refuse-with-redirect templates for the conversational surface
- no-claim-approval-by-worker rule (explicit, unconditional)
- no-customer-facing-regulated-copy rule (explicit, unconditional)
- claim-gate exit verdicts and their downstream effect on the blog and social workflows
- evidence and source map for every load-bearing claim in this artifact
- reflection and learning proposals

Excluded:

- making, drafting, approving, or publishing any claim (regulated or otherwise)
- legal counsel or licensed professional advice
- medical, Rx, financial or security advisory
- customer data access of any form
- external posting, scheduling, outreach
- spend changes
- production deployment
- Plane state mutation by the worker (no Done, no status flips)
- country-specific regulatory analysis (national law differences are flagged to
  Founder; this spec treats all regulated claims as requiring Founder review)

## 3. Regulated Claim Classes

### 3.1 Class Table

Six regulated claim classes are defined. Every class has a stable machine-readable
identifier, a description, examples of in-scope claims, and a minimum evidence
requirement. The minimum evidence must be satisfied before the draft may advance
from the claim gate to any subsequent stage or queue state.

| # | Class ID | Description | In-Scope Examples | Out-Of-Scope Examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | `medical` | Claims about health outcomes, clinical efficacy, disease prevention or treatment, physiological benefits, or medical safety | "Reduces anxiety by 40%", "clinically proven to improve sleep", "treats chronic pain", "FDA approved for X" | "supports a healthy lifestyle" (without clinical backing), "sleep tracking feature" |
| C2 | `rx` | Claims about prescription drugs, controlled substances, Rx-only devices, or any statement that could be read as a dosing, prescribing, or contraindication claim | "Take two capsules for…", "equivalent efficacy to [Rx drug]", "safe to combine with [medication]" | "caffeine for alertness" without dosing claims |
| C3 | `legal` | Claims about legal compliance, regulatory status, liability limitation, contract enforceability, or any statement implying legal standing | "GDPR compliant", "fully compliant with…", "not liable for…", "meets all regulatory requirements in…", "ISO 27001 certified" | "we take privacy seriously" (without certification claim) |
| C4 | `financial` | Claims about financial returns, ROI guarantees, investment performance, revenue projections, or any forward-looking financial promise | "guaranteed 3× ROI", "average customer saves EUR X", "outperforms the market", "projected revenue growth of Y%" | "pricing starts at EUR Z" (factual pricing), "case study shows one customer saved X" (attributed, not guaranteed) |
| C5 | `security` | Claims about security guarantees, audit certifications, zero-vulnerability assertions, data breach prevention, or encryption strength | "breach-proof", "zero vulnerabilities", "SOC 2 Type II certified", "end-to-end encrypted" (without current cert path), "our systems cannot be hacked" | "we use TLS for data in transit" (verifiable fact) |
| C6 | `privacy` | Claims about data retention policies, data sale/sharing prohibitions, GDPR/CCPA specific compliance assertions, or anonymization guarantees | "we never sell your data" (as a guarantee binding future behavior), "data deleted within X days always", "fully GDPR compliant in all EU jurisdictions" | "you may request data deletion" (policy statement with documented process) |

`FACT(docs/releases/0.6a-marketing-growth-command-center-map.md)` Section 5 —
the parent command center map lists "medical, legal, Rx, financial outcome claims"
as blocked-action class examples. This table extends the list with explicit
`security` and `privacy` classes, consistent with the scope in
`docs/strategy/autonomy-product-horizon.md` Section 10 (Finance, Legal, Security
And Compliance area).

`INFERENCE(docs/releases/0.6b-blog-workflow-approval-packet.md)` Section 6 —
the blog workflow Section 6 names "medical, legal, Rx, financial outcome, security,
and any other regulated class" as examples; this spec enumerates all six classes
so "any other" has a deterministic fallback (see §3.2 below).

### 3.2 Unclassified-Claim Fallback Rule

If a claim does not clearly fit any of C1–C6, the gate worker applies this
decision tree:

1. **Does the claim make a promise the company could be held legally or financially
   liable for if it proves false?** → treat as `C3 legal` and route to HG-4.
2. **Does the claim imply a health or safety outcome?** → treat as `C1 medical`
   and route to HG-4.
3. **Does the claim imply a data protection or privacy guarantee?** → treat as
   `C6 privacy` and route to HG-4.
4. **None of the above, but the claim is factually unsupported in the current
   draft?** → STALE or UNSUPPORTED verdict; return to writer for evidence before
   the gate can pass.

If the gate worker cannot resolve the classification with confidence, the claim
is escalated to HG-4 (Founder) as unclassified-regulated. No worker may pass
an ambiguous claim without Founder sign-off.

## 4. Minimum Evidence Requirements Per Class

Before any draft may pass the claim gate, every claim in the draft must meet
the minimum evidence requirement for its class. Evidence must be present in the
claim review artifact (`05-claim-review.md` for blog, `claim-review.md` for
social) before the gate worker issues any verdict other than REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED.

| Class ID | Minimum Evidence Required | Evidence Freshness Window | Evidence Acceptable Forms |
|---|---|---|---|
| `medical` | Peer-reviewed study or official clinical trial result with full citation (author, year, journal, DOI or URL); OR official regulatory body decision with case number | Published within 5 years unless declared "evergreen" by the Founder; clinical trials must be current (ongoing or completed; not retracted) | DOI link + abstract quote; regulatory decision document path; note: Honcho memory is NOT acceptable evidence |
| `rx` | Same as `medical` PLUS licensed healthcare provider or pharmacist statement if the claim involves dosing, contraindication or drug interaction | Same as `medical` | Same as `medical`; licensed provider name and credential required for dosing claims |
| `legal` | Official regulatory body confirmation (certification number, audit report, dated decision), OR external legal counsel written opinion (named counsel, firm, date, jurisdiction) | Certification: must be currently valid (not expired); legal opinion: within 12 months | Certification document path with expiry date; counsel letter path or signed memo path |
| `financial` | Audited financial data or attributed case study with methodology note, clearly labeled as historical performance, not a guarantee; OR actuary-reviewed projection with confidence interval | Historical: within 2 years; projections: must include forward-looking disclaimer drafted by Founder | Audit report path or case study document path; disclaimer text must be present in the draft alongside the claim |
| `security` | Current certification document with scope and expiry (e.g., SOC 2 audit report, ISO 27001 cert), OR third-party penetration test report with date and scope; no assertion may extend beyond the documented scope | Certification must be current; pen-test within 12 months | Cert document path with expiry; pen-test report path with scope declaration |
| `privacy` | Current privacy policy document path plus legal counsel confirmation that the specific claim is accurate as of the policy's effective date; OR data-protection impact assessment for GDPR-specific claims | Privacy policy effective date must be current; DPIA within 12 months | Policy document path; counsel confirmation letter path or internal legal note path |
| Unclassified-regulated | Same as the most restrictive applicable class, or full Founder review if classification is ambiguous | Same as the applicable class | Founder written confirmation in a Plane comment or signed document |

`FACT(docs/templates/worker-issue-contract.md)` — HG-4 remains Founder-owned for
public regulated medical/legal/Rx/financial claims, material spend/pricing and
similar high-risk actions. This table extends that doctrine with security and
privacy certifications, consistent with the rule that claims which could expose
the company to external legal or reputational liability require Founder ownership.

`INFERENCE(docs/orchestration/claude-clevel-worker-runtime.md)` (referenced from
`AGENTS.md` line 72–75) — Honcho memory is proposal-only for C-level workers;
it is therefore not acceptable as evidence for any regulated claim. Evidence must
be a verifiable artifact with a path or URL.

## 5. Role Ownership And HG-4 Escalation Map

### 5.1 Claim Ownership Table

| Class ID | Primary Owner | Secondary Owner | HG-4 (Founder) Required? | When HG-4 is Mandatory |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `medical` | Founder | `role:cao` (audit) | **Always** | HG-4 is the only path to claim approval; no CMO override |
| `rx` | Founder | `role:cao` (audit) | **Always** | Same as `medical`; Rx claims may not pass without Founder sign |
| `legal` | Founder | `role:cao` (audit) + external counsel | **Always** | Legal compliance claims must have Founder + counsel confirmation |
| `financial` | Founder | `role:cfo` (budget review) + `role:cao` (audit) | **Always** | Forward-looking financial claims; historical with methodology → Founder still signs |
| `security` | Founder | `role:cao` (audit) + `role:cto` (technical review) | **Always** | All C5 security claims require HG-4. Purely factual implementation statements may be classified as `standard` only when they include a current evidence path and make no guarantee, certification, compliance or breach-prevention assertion |
| `privacy` | Founder | `role:cao` (audit) + external counsel | **Always** | GDPR/CCPA specific assertions; data-deletion guarantees; "we never sell" type claims |
| `standard` | `role:cmo` | `role:cao` (audit for freshness/source) | **No** (HG-2 sufficient) | Only when the claim is verifiably factual, sourced, fresh and no regulated class applies |
| Unclassified-regulated | Founder | `role:cao` (audit) | **Always** | Classification ambiguity requires Founder resolution; no worker may self-classify upward to `standard` |

### 5.2 What "HG-4 Mandatory" Means In This Gate

When HG-4 is mandatory:

1. The gate worker (`role:cao`) produces a `claim-review.md` or
   `05-claim-review.md` artifact with `overall_verdict: REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED`
   and `hg4_escalation_required: true`.
2. The upstream workflow (blog S5 or social `claim_review`) pauses. The draft
   MUST NOT advance to the next stage or queue state.
3. The Plane work item for the draft receives a `claim_gate.hg3_escalation`
   comment with the claim log shape defined in Section 6.
4. Founder reviews the specific claim, its evidence, and the proposed draft copy
   in Plane.
5. Founder may:
   - **Approve** the claim with a `claim_gate.founder_approved` Plane comment
     and a signed evidence path. The gate worker then re-checks evidence
     freshness and issues `overall_verdict: PASS` if all criteria are met.
   - **Reject** the claim. The draft returns to the writer for revision or
     removal of the claim.
   - **Request additional evidence.** The gate pauses until evidence is
     supplied and the CAO re-runs the gate.
6. No agent, no CMO, no CEO may substitute for Founder on HG-4 claims. CEO
   HG-2.5 release authority does not extend to regulated claim approval.

`FACT(docs/governance/ceo-release-authority.md)` (referenced from `AGENTS.md`
line 104) — HG-2.5 CEO release authority covers bounded merge/deploy/production
actions; it does not cover regulated claim approval, which remains HG-4.

## 6. Claim-Review Log Shape (Plane Comment)

Every claim review that passes through this gate MUST produce a structured log
in the Plane work item for the draft being reviewed. The log is posted as a
Plane comment by the `role:cao` seat after the review completes.

### 6.1 Claim-Review Log YAML

```yaml
claim_review_log:
  version: cg-v0
  work_item: <Plane work item ID for the draft being reviewed>
  reviewer_seat: role:cao
  reviewed_at: <ISO-8601 UTC>
  artifact_path: <absolute path to the claim-review.md artifact in this repo>
  claims_reviewed:
    - claim_id: <unique stable ID, e.g. "c001">
      claim_text: "<quoted claim text as it appears in the draft>"
      claim_class: medical | rx | legal | financial | security | privacy | standard | unclassified-regulated
      evidence_path: <absolute path or URL to the primary evidence document>
      evidence_type: peer-reviewed-study | regulatory-decision | legal-opinion | audit-report | pen-test | policy-document | case-study | founder-confirmation | none
      evidence_freshness: <ISO-8601 publication/certification date, or "evergreen">
      evidence_expiry: <ISO-8601 expiry date if applicable, or "none">
      verdict: PASS | REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED | REGULATED_CLAIM_APPROVED | CLAIM_REMOVED | REJECT | STALE | UNSUPPORTED
      hg3_required: true | false
      notes: "<one-line explanation of the verdict, required if verdict is not PASS>"
  overall_verdict: PASS | REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED | REGULATED_CLAIM_APPROVED | CLAIM_REMOVED | REJECT | STALE | UNSUPPORTED
  hg4_escalation_required: true | false
  hg4_founder_approved: false | <Plane comment ID of founder approval>
  next_action: advance | pause-hg3 | return-to-writer
  gate_version: 0.6d
```

### 6.2 Evidence Path Convention

All claim-review artifacts are stored under:

```text
reports/marketing-drafts/<slug>/05-claim-review.md       (blog workflow, Stage S5)
reports/social-drafts/<slug>/claim-review.md             (social workflow, claim_review state)
```

The `<slug>` is the lowercased kebab-case of the draft title, consistent with
the convention defined in `docs/releases/0.6b-blog-workflow-approval-packet.md`
Section 3.2.

`HYPOTHESIS` — `reports/social-drafts/<slug>/` is a proposed path for the social
workflow; it is not yet written by this run and must be declared in downstream
child Worker Contracts for the social workflow. The blog path
(`reports/marketing-drafts/<slug>/`) inherits directly from 0.6B Section 3.2.

### 6.3 Log Completeness Rule

The claim-review log is only valid when:

1. Every claim in the draft that could be regulated has an entry in
   `claims_reviewed`. A log with zero entries is not valid unless the draft
   genuinely contains no factual claims (e.g., a question-only or navigation-only
   piece).
2. Every entry has a non-empty `evidence_path` or a `verdict` of UNSUPPORTED.
   An entry may not carry `verdict: PASS` with an empty evidence path.
3. `overall_verdict` matches the most severe per-claim verdict: any
   REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED in the per-claim list → `overall_verdict:
   REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED`; any REJECT without REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED →
   `overall_verdict: REJECT`; all PASS → `overall_verdict: PASS`.
4. `hg4_escalation_required` is `true` if any claim carries `hg3_required: true`.

## 7. Refuse-With-Redirect Templates

The conversational surface (`0.6A Marketing/Growth Command Center`) MUST use
the following templates when a regulated claim is encountered in a chat session.
No paraphrase. Templates are reproduced verbatim so the surface can match them
deterministically.

### 7.1 Claim Detected In Draft

When a worker or conversational surface detects a regulated claim in a draft
during a chat session:

> "This draft contains a regulated claim (class: `{class_id}`). Regulated claims
> require a dedicated Claim/Compliance Gate review (0.6D) with Founder approval
> (HG-4) before this draft may move past the claim review stage. I am pausing
> this draft at the claim gate. I have not approved, modified or removed the
> claim. The next step is for the Founder to review the claim and its evidence
> path in Plane. Routing to the claim gate now."

### 7.2 Request To Approve A Regulated Claim From Chat

When an operator asks the conversational surface to approve a regulated claim:

> "I cannot approve regulated claims. Claim approval for class `{class_id}` is
> reserved for the Founder (HG-4) per the Company.OS 0.6D Claim/Compliance Gate
> spec. No worker, no CMO, and no CEO can substitute for Founder approval on
> regulated claims. Please route this claim through the Plane Claim/Compliance
> Gate workflow."

### 7.3 Request To Push A Regulated Draft Past The Gate Without HG-4

When an operator asks the surface to continue with a draft that has an unresolved
REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED verdict:

> "I cannot advance this draft. The Claim/Compliance Gate has returned
> `REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED` with `hg4_escalation_required: true`. The draft
> must stay paused at the claim review stage until the Founder approves or the
> claim is removed by the writer. Bypassing this gate is explicitly forbidden."

### 7.4 Request To Draft Regulated Copy For Customer-Facing Publication

When an operator asks the conversational surface to draft customer-facing copy
that would contain regulated claims:

> "I cannot draft customer-facing regulated copy. Drafting copy containing
> medical, Rx, legal, financial, security or privacy claims that are intended
> for external publication requires Founder-reviewed evidence before the draft
> enters the writing stage. I can help identify what evidence would be needed
> for this type of claim. Please open a Plane work item for this evidence
> research and route it through the Claim/Compliance Gate before drafting."

### 7.5 Refusal Without A Specific Class (General Regulated Claim Refusal)

When the surface cannot classify the claim but suspects it is regulated:

> "This claim may be regulated (unclassified). I cannot confirm or draft copy
> containing it without Founder review. Treating this as an escalation to HG-4.
> Please route through the Claim/Compliance Gate in Plane."

## 8. No-Claim-Approval-By-Worker Rule

**Absolute rule, no exceptions:**

No worker, no agent, no conversational surface, no `role:cmo` seat, no `role:cao`
seat, and no CEO/Codex controller operating under Company.OS may approve a
regulated claim (classes C1–C6 and unclassified-regulated) without explicit
Founder sign-off at HG-4.

This rule applies regardless of:

- how many prior approved claims exist in the company's history
- whether the claim is similar to a previously Founder-approved claim (each
  claim is reviewed independently; blanket approval is not valid)
- whether the evidence is strong or the claim is "obviously true"
- whether the CEO or `role:cmo` seat judges the claim to be low-risk
- whether the draft is for internal use only (regulated classes still require
  Founder review if the content could become public-facing)

CAO's role at the claim gate is **audit and routing only**: enumerate claims,
classify them, check evidence completeness, and route to Founder if HG-4 is
required. CAO does not approve, remove, or paraphrase regulated claims.

`FACT(docs/agents/cao.md)` — CAO does not build, does not edit, does not approve.
CAO transitions a work item to `ceo:review` on PASS or back to the building seat
on REJECT; it does not write regulated copy or approve regulated content.

## 9. No-Customer-Facing-Regulated-Copy Rule

**Absolute rule, no exceptions:**

No worker, no agent, no conversational surface, and no automated workflow
operating under Company.OS may produce customer-facing copy that contains
regulated claims unless:

1. The specific claim has been reviewed through this gate (0.6D).
2. The specific claim carries `hg4_founder_approved: <comment ID>` in the claim
   review log.
3. The claim text in the approved customer-facing copy matches the
   Founder-approved claim text exactly (no paraphrase, no shortening, no
   recontextualization without a new gate run).
4. The evidence cited in the claim is still current (within the freshness window
   for its class).
5. The final draft with the approved claim has passed all editorial stages and
   HG-2.5 release (for blog) or the scheduling intent card flow (for social)
   before any customer-facing publication.

A claim that was approved in one draft does not carry over automatically to a
new draft. Every new draft containing a regulated claim must go through the gate.

`FACT(docs/releases/0.6a-marketing-growth-command-center-map.md)` Section 5 —
"Regulated/public claims: medical, legal, Rx, financial outcome claims: HG-4
(Founder) + Claim/Compliance Gate child (Section 6.3)." This rule is a direct
operationalization of that blocked-action class.

## 10. Claim-Gate Exit Verdicts And Downstream Effect

Every claim gate run produces exactly one of these exit verdicts. The verdict
determines what happens to the upstream workflow.

| Exit Verdict | Meaning | Downstream Effect |
|---|---|---|
| `PASS` | All claims in the draft are in class `standard`, are supported by verifiable evidence, and are fresh. No regulated classes detected. | Blog: draft advances from S5 to S6 `staged_publish`. Social: queue advances from `claim_review` to `scheduling_intent`. |
| `REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED` | One or more claims fall into C1–C6 or unclassified-regulated. Gate is paused pending Founder review. | Blog: draft pauses at S5. Social: draft pauses at `claim_review`. In both cases: `hg4_escalation_required: true` in the log; Founder receives the Plane escalation comment. |
| `REGULATED_CLAIM_APPROVED` | Founder (HG-4) has signed the claim. Evidence is verified fresh. Gate worker has confirmed evidence is complete. | Blog: draft may advance from S5 to S6. Social: draft may advance from `claim_review` to `scheduling_intent`. The log entry carries `hg4_founder_approved: <comment ID>`. |
| `CLAIM_REMOVED` | Writer has removed the regulated claim from the draft. Gate worker has verified the removal and the draft no longer contains regulated content. | Gate re-runs on the revised draft. If the revised draft carries only `standard` claims with sufficient evidence, `PASS` is issued. |
| `REJECT` | Evidence is missing, stale, or the draft contains a claim that neither the writer nor the Founder could substantiate. | Blog: draft returns to S3 (`draft`) for revision. Social: draft returns to `draft` state. Reason documented in the claim-review artifact. |
| `STALE` | Evidence exists but is outside the freshness window for its class. | Same as REJECT; writer must supply fresh evidence or remove the claim. |
| `UNSUPPORTED` | Claim has no evidence path at all. | Same as REJECT; draft may not advance. |

## 13. Reflection

`reflection:`

- The report-only mode (CAO/governance, not CMO/builder) mapped cleanly: this
  artifact defines the gate rules without making, drafting, approving, or routing
  any specific claim. Every template, every rule, every table is a specification
  of what the gate will do when claims arrive — not a demonstration of claim
  approval.
- The hardest design tension was the `security` and `privacy` class extension.
  The parent command center map (0.6A) names only medical/legal/Rx/financial as
  examples. Adding security and privacy as explicit classes was an inference from
  `docs/strategy/autonomy-product-horizon.md` Section 10 (which places security
  and privacy certifications in the HG-4 zone). This inference is flagged as
  INFERENCE in the source map and could be challenged by Founder at HG-4 review.
  If Founder narrowing is required, C5 and C6 can be merged into C3 (legal) or
  removed; the gate structure is unchanged.
- The freshness windows (Section 4) are the only data points in this spec that
  carry no Company.OS file evidence. They are industry-norm inferences and are
  therefore filed as Learning Proposal #2 for explicit Founder calibration. Any
  Founder override of these windows supersedes this spec immediately.
- The no-claim-approval-by-worker rule (Section 8) and the no-customer-facing-
  regulated-copy rule (Section 9) are stated in absolute terms because any
  softening ("except when...") would create a precedent for workers to self-
  classify claims downward to `standard`. The rules are unconditional by design.
- The claim-gate exit verdict table (Section 10) provides six distinct outcomes
  to avoid the ambiguity that comes from a binary PASS/FAIL: `STALE`,
  `UNSUPPORTED`, `CLAIM_REMOVED` and `REGULATED_CLAIM_APPROVED` each describe a
  different state the gate and the draft can be in, which lets the upstream
  workflow track exactly where the draft is without re-querying the gate.
- The 0.6D role is `role:cao` with `role:cmo` as secondary for ordinary copy.
  This maintains the CAO/builder separation: CMO builds and reviews ordinary copy;
  CAO audits claims; Founder approves regulated ones. No seat collapses into another.

## 14. Learning Proposals

`learning_proposals:` (proposal-only; no durable memory, SOP, skill, workflow
or harness change happens without controller/CEO review)

1. **proposal:** Extract the claim-review log YAML shape (Section 6.1) into a
   reusable template at `docs/templates/claim-review-log.md` so any future
   workflow that needs a claim gate (editorial controller rubric in 0.6F, analytics
   loop in 0.6E) can inherit the same `cg-v0` schema without re-defining it.
   Risk: premature abstraction before multiple claim-gate runs prove the schema
   stable. Rollback: delete the template; claim-review log stays inline in this
   spec.

2. **proposal:** Have Founder explicitly calibrate the evidence freshness windows
   (Section 4: 5yr medical, 12mo legal/security, 2yr financial) in a signed
   document or Plane comment against this spec. These windows are industry-norm
   inferences; they may need to be tighter (e.g., 3yr medical for fast-moving
   fields) or looser (e.g., legal opinions in stable jurisdictions) depending on
   the company's actual regulatory exposure. Rollback: revert to these defaults
   if no calibration is provided within 30 days of Founder review.

3. **proposal:** Add a `claim_class_registry` file at
   `docs/governance/claim-class-registry.md` that allows the Founder to extend,
   merge, or narrow the six claim classes (C1–C6) without modifying this spec
   document. This would decouple the gate logic from the class taxonomy, allowing
   the taxonomy to evolve as the company enters new regulated domains (e.g., HR,
   hiring, medical devices). Risk: divergence between registry and this spec
   if both are updated independently. Rollback: delete the registry; classes
   remain defined in this spec.

4. **proposal:** Add a soft `claim_gate.classification_ambiguous` advisory
   comment shape to the Plane log so the CAO seat can flag unclassified-regulated
   claims without issuing a hard REJECT. Currently the only options are
   REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED (which pauses the draft) and a REJECT (which returns
   it). A soft advisory would allow the Founder to classify the claim without
   the draft losing its editorial and claim-review progress up to the ambiguous
   point. Risk: operators may treat the advisory as a soft PASS. Rollback:
   remove the advisory shape; keep binary REGULATED_CLAIM_DETECTED/REJECT.

5. **proposal:** Cross-reference this gate with the planned 0.6F Editorial
   Controller Rubric (`docs/releases/0.6-growth-beta-entry-plan.md` Suggested
   Child Contracts line 6) so the editorial controller's CAO pass explicitly
   checks that S5 / `claim_review` artifacts exist and carry a non-UNSUPPORTED
   verdict before issuing a controller PASS on any blog or social item. Risk:
   coupling the editorial rubric to this gate prematurely before 0.6F is defined.
   Rollback: keep the two specs independent; editorial controller re-runs the
   freshness check without requiring a claim gate artifact.
